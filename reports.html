Feb, 2025

National Quantum Strategy Roadmap-QUANTUM COMPUTING:Canada

The Canadian National Quantum Strategy (NQS) has committed $360 million to secure the country's position as a global leader in quantum computing hardware and software development and deployment. The long-term mission is to build a scalable fault-tolerant quantum computer (targeting 100 logical qubits) by urgently addressing key challenges, including securing a domestic supply chain for critical components and developing quantum error correction codes.

South Korea has become a highly advanced and flexible player in the global quantum field. QUANTUM: THE TECHNOLOGICAL SHIFT THAT FRANCE WON'T MISS

December, 2024

China’s Quantum Computing and Quantum Technology Initiatives

China's massive, state-led push in quantum technology, backed by an estimated $15 billion in public funds, aims to achieve global leadership across multiple domains. They are the clear global frontrunner in Quantum Communications (QKD), demonstrated by the world's first satellite, Micius, and extensive terrestrial networks. In Quantum Computing, China has achieved "quantum advantage" with photonic (Jiuzhang 3.0) and superconducting (Zuchongzhi 2.0) systems, rapidly scaling up hardware and challenging Western rivals.
