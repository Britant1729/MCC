August, 2025

South Korea: State of Quantum

South Korea has become a highly advanced and flexible player in the global quantum field. Building on its strength in semiconductors and 5G infrastructure, the country is now using its national research and corporate R&D to speed up quantum technologies—especially in quantum sensing, communications, and hybrid chip designs. As of August 2025, South Korea is moving from pilot projects to a full ecosystem strategy, with growing international cooperation and export opportunities..

May, 2025

State of the art of fault-tolerant quantum computing questions and challenges- France

This report by the French Academy of Technologies evaluates the feasibility and impact of Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing (FTQC), confirming its theoretical advantage for certain problems (like factorization and simulation), but highlighting that French industrial use cases are currently poorly identified.

April, 2023

Quantum Technologies Conceptual Framework Programme-Germany

Germany's Conceptual Framework for Quantum Technologies targets establishing the nation and Europe as global leaders by 2026, aiming for technological sovereignty and increased value creation. This initiative prioritizes developing standards, supporting start-ups, and fostering a specialized workforce to secure Germany's long-term position in this foundational field.

January, 2020

QUANTUM: THE TECHNOLOGICAL SHIFT THAT FRANCE WON'T MISS

The French parliamentary report identifies a need for an ambitious national strategy, as France's excellent quantum research is lagging in industrial and technological development, threatening its sovereignty. The core ambition is to become a major global quantum industrial power this decade, targeting world leadership in Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computers and European leadership in NISQ systems.

December, 2024

China’s Quantum Computing and Quantum Technology Initiatives

China's massive, state-led push in quantum technology, backed by an estimated $15 billion in public funds, aims to achieve global leadership across multiple domains. They are the clear global frontrunner in Quantum Communications (QKD), demonstrated by the world's first satellite, Micius, and extensive terrestrial networks. In Quantum Computing, China has achieved "quantum advantage" with photonic (Jiuzhang 3.0) and superconducting (Zuchongzhi 2.0) systems, rapidly scaling up hardware and challenging Western rivals.

July, 2025

Quantum Europe Strategy

The Quantum Europe Strategy aims to transform the continent into a global industrial powerhouse by overcoming fragmentation and capitalizing on its scientific excellence. The plan is structured around five key areas—Research & Innovation, Infrastructure, Ecosystem, Security (Dual-Use), and Skills—to accelerate industrial deployment and ensure strategic autonomy.
